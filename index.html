Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Innovation is the process of creating new ideas, methods, or products that bring improvement, efficiency, and transformative change.

Sustainability

Sustainability is the practice of meeting present needs while preserving resources, minimizing environmental impact, and ensuring future generations' well-being.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure comprises essential facilities, systems, and structures like roads, bridges, and utilities, enabling economic growth, connectivity, and societal development.

Empowerment

Empowerment is the process of giving individuals or communities the tools, confidence to make decisions and drive change.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transmission

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens in the country.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"Each individual holds the potential to create meaningful change and shape a better world. Poverty is not a natural state; it is a failure of systems and institutions that prioritize profit over people. We must challenge these systems and believe in the power of innovative solutions to transform lives. Empowering people, especially the marginalized, through tools like microcredit, education, and sustainable development can unlock their inherent capabilities and dignity. Poverty does not belong in civilized human society. It belongs in museums. Our challenge is not just to reduce poverty but to eradicate it entirely by building inclusive systems that serve humanity as a whole."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
